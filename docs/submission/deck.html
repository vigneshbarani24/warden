The problem

Valid in the ERP.
Illegal in the business.

An AI agent books a trade — then approves the settlement on the deal it just booked. A segregation-of-duties breach, executed at machine speed, with no record of why it was allowed.

for finance & risk operators
Why now

The controls were never in the ERP.
They were in the latency.

  • A human had to click. Routing a transaction took a day across two desks.
  • Agents erase that delay — and the control vanishes with it.
  • The gap is invisible: every action looks valid, so nothing alarms — and it compounds with every agent you deploy.
What Warden is

A decision point before. A sealed record after.

Before the action — the PDP

The agent posts a proposed action. Warden returns allow · deny · escalate, with a human-readable reason and the rule ids that fired.

After — the ledger

Every verdict is appended to a hash-chained, append-only ledger. Tamper-evident: any edit is detectable, and provable.

No foreign keys, no sequences — integrity enforced in-transaction; ids are client UUIDs. The grant lock is what makes a concurrent revoke abort instead of approving stale.

▶ Live — why Amazon Aurora DSQL

Revoked here. Denied there. Instantly.

Allow a $2M trade in us-east-1 → revoke the mandate → the identical action in us-west-2 is denied, with no stale read.

The only AWS-native database giving synchronous, active-active strong consistency and the SELECT … FOR UPDATE transaction this decision needs.
